A computer science undergraduate passionate about CS research

Introduction

I'm a third year at the University of Virginia, double majoring in computer science and Japanese. I spent the past summer interning in the Seattle area with Microsoft Artificial Intelligence & Research.

I've always loved teaching and have been a TA for three semesters (both first-year Japanese and an introductory CS class in C++). I've been a CS research assistant for four semesters now, and I plan on earning a PhD in CS and getting tenure in a research university.

Outside of School

I also love cycling, ice skating, and baking. I enjoy many creative things, like 3D printing, photography, and embroidery. One of my goals is to visit all 50 states before I turn 30! Only 28 to go :)

Research interests include:

Machine Learning

When I declared as a CS major, I wanted to learn about Natural Language Processing (NLP)--- but I soon realized there are lots of other cool things you can do with machine learning!

Human Computer Interaction

If people are the end users of the machines and programs that CS majors make, shouldn't we design for the users? I love UI/UX, usability, and accessability. I believe CS has the power to make the world a more inclusive place.

CS Education

I love teaching and believe that professors can change the world one student at a time. Good teaching and good TAs make a difference! I'm hoping to attend the ACM SIGCSE conference in 2019.

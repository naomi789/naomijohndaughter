Style guide

For family and individual portrait sessions

The clothes you choose to wear can really make or break your portrait session! Most importantly, pick clothes that you feel comfortable and confident in. Here are some tips on how to pick clothes that will make you look good, too!

  1. Choose a color scheme

    2. I recommend choosing three or four photos that are all in the same set of tones (earth tones, blue tones, dark jewel tones, pastels, neutrals, etc) and then having everyone in your group pick outfits that are within this scheme. You don't need to match perfectly, you need to cordinate!

  2. Wear solid colored clothing

    3. Busy prints can be distracting and make it hard to pick matching outfits for the rest of your family, so make sure no more than one person is wearing patterns! Avoid clothing with words, logos, or characters, unless you're bringing a school sweatshirt to your graduation photoshoot. When people look at the photos, they will be trying to read the writing instead of actually looking at your photos. Instead of patterns, try experiementing with textures!

  3. Shoes

    4. Wear something you're comfortable in: avoid new shoes that might give you blisters because you'll likely be doing a bit of walking between backgrounds! Make sure the shoes match the setting: if your portrait session is at the beach, stilettos might look out of place. Gentlemen, consider polishing your shoes. Parents, do a shoe check before you get in your car--- it's no fun to get to the site and then realize your toddler is wearing rainboots with their suit!

  4. Bring accessories!

    5. Bring a hat, vest, scarf, or sweater! These are easy to take on and off, and will give your photos more variety. Jewellery is nice too, but remember to keep it simple so the attention is on you, instead of what you're wearing.

  5. Glasses

    6. I recommend taking your glasses off 15-20 minutes before your session so their is time for the marks on your nose have time to disappear. If you plan on wearing them, I'll work with you to minimize reflection and glare!

  6. Hairstyling and nails

    7. If you're going to get your haircut, do it at least two weeks before your session so you're more used to it! Think about your outfit before you pick a hairstyle: evening gowns and ponytails go as well together as tshirts and fancy up dos. If it's windy or you are prone to fly-away hair, bobby pins or hair ties might be helpful to bring in case you put your hair up. You don't need to go get a manicure for your portrait session, but make sure that your nails look well-trimmed and clean and the color doesn't detract from or clash with your outfits!

  7. Seek inspiration and ask questions!

    8. Check out Pinterest or other social media if you need more inspiration! Feel free to message me a photo of your outfits. If you don't feel comfortable doing that, ask a friend who has great style--- they'll probably take it as a compliment :)
